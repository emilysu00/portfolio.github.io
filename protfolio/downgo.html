Mountain First Aid

（ongoing）

Team：Su Yenhua, Wu Yijing, Chen Yujie, Li Chenyu

Using pixel art and interactive gaming methods to convey essential information for mountain climbing and emergency self-rescue. The goal is to present complex and serious information in a fun and engaging way, aiming to make it easier for readers to understand.
web Link
Web design

Introduction

Use typing animation to briefly narrate the main theme of the webpage, similar to a preface in a book.

Index

Incorporate simple mouse movement to add a subtle dynamic effect to the Key Vision.

Preparation - Hiking Equipment

Break down the layers of the backpack, allowing users to click on different categories to view the items needed in each layer.

Preparation - Wound Dressing

Present various body parts in clickable frames, demonstrating different bandaging methods. Clicking on each frame will lead to a simple interactive simulation of bandaging.

Preparation - Entrance

Choose to enter different paths.

Mountain Climbing Safety Tips

Enable users to climb a mountain by sliding or clicking on "Advance" or "Escape" buttons. As the slide progresses, the altitude above will change, corresponding to the dangers encountered at different altitudes. Clicking on different monsters will display information about them in the lower-left information box.

graphic design

Including a small booklet, fracture splint, hiking trail markers, bandages, and outer box design. Utilizing the booklet to convey essential mountain climbing instructions and combining it with items for emergency situations. Designing the appearance with easily understandable visual instructions for users' convenience.
